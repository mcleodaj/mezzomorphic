Project Goals

Song Analysis

Going into this project, there was a clear idea of our network being able to analyze a song file, and the rest of our project was based entirely on this analysis.

Music Generation

Our next goal was having the ability to not only analyze the tracks passed into our network, but to then be able to have our network generate its own "music" based on what it was hearing.

File I/O

Finally we aimed to have a system for users to easily pass songs into our network, and access the outputs once analysis and generation had been run.

Our Final Project Progress

  • Thursday and Friday

    Research, Research, Research

    One of the most difficult aspects of this project was the fact that no one had truly touched machine learning before. There had been some exposure through the deeplearning group at DBC, but we had never written any sort of neural network code. We all buried our heads in material on neural networks and learned an incredible amount

  • Saturday

    A neural network is born

    After slowly expanding our knowledge and working through countless basic tutorials, we finally began to construct our neural network. We achieved a functional network Saturday afternoon, but with the large caveat of the network could only learn, and did not have the ability to generate any noise yet.

  • Sunday and Monday

    Pivots, Concerns, and Frustrations

    As we started to realize the constraints of our neural network and the library it was built on, frustrations began to emerge. We spiked for over 6 hours on trying to determine if it was worth learning an entire new library and reconstructing our entire network for the sake of easy generation, but instead decided to stick with what we knew. After countless hours of research we achieved our first generation early Monday morning!

  • Tuesday through Thursday

    Training, Training, Training

    The time for our final project was quickly coming to a close, and we had to overcome several hurdles just to get the one decent beat we can show you today. Training on sound files took an average of 4-5 hours for anything around 15 seconds in link with 5 training sessions. We were getting terrible initial results with these sessions as well, until we began to implement dropout, adjust some of our node numbers, and tweak our networks parameters.
